## People to AVOID (Season 01)

‚ùå **The Expert (2200+ rated)**
- Will dominate conversations with advanced concepts
- Makes beginners feel inadequate
- Not your target user ‚Äì save for "advanced track" later

‚ùå **The Chronic Ghoster**
- Always says "yes, yes, I'll join" then disappears
- You'll waste energy following up
- Better to have 5 committed than 10 half-in

‚ùå **The Overwhelmed Parent/Founder**
- Extremely busy and stressed
- Will feel guilty for not participating
- You'll feel bad "bothering" them
- Invite them to Season 02 when life calms down

‚ùå **The "Let Me Tell You Why This Won't Work" Guy**
- Immediately suggests massive pivots
- Wants to "help you build features" instead of testing what exists
- You need testers, not co-founders

‚ùå **The Unintentional Discourager**
- Says things like "chess is easy, just don't blunder"
- Makes beginners feel dumb without meaning to
- Vibe killer for your calm club

## Selection Checklist

Before finalizing your list, verify:

**Diversity of Experience:**
- [ ] At least 2 true beginners (primary target)
- [ ] At least 1 honest friend (brutal feedback)
- [ ] Mix of "played before" and "never really played"

**Diversity of Personality:**
- [ ] At least 1 quiet person (self-paced tester)
- [ ] At least 1 chatty person (will engage in Discord)
- [ ] Mix of analytical and intuitive thinkers

**Practical Fit:**
- [ ] Everyone can commit to 20-30 min/week for 4 weeks
- [ ] Everyone is comfortable with alpha/rough edges
- [ ] Everyone knows you personally (or via warm intro)

**Geographic/Timezone (Bonus):**
- [ ] Mix of timezones (tests async club model)
- [ ] At least 1 non-US member (if going international)

**You don't need perfect diversity. 5 committed humans > 10 perfect-on-paper ghosts.**

## What to Do If You Get "No" or Ghost

**Expected and okay:**
- Aim for 10 invites ‚Üí expect 5-7 to join ‚Üí expect 3-5 to stay active throughout
- If 5 people complete all 4 weeks, that's a massive win

**If someone says no:**
- Response: "No worries at all! I'll let you know when Season 02 launches if you want to join then."
- Add them to "Season 02 potential members" list

**If someone ghosts (doesn't respond):**
- Wait 48 hours
- Send one gentle follow-up: "Hey! Just checking if you got my message about Chessio. No pressure either way!"
- If still no response ‚Üí move on, don't take it personally

**If someone joins but doesn't start:**
- Wait until Wednesday (2 days after launch)
- Send private DM: "Hey! Noticed you joined Discord but haven't started the mission yet. Any blockers? Totally fine if timing doesn't work!"
- If they say timing's bad ‚Üí "No problem, maybe Season 02?"

## Mental Framework: Quality > Quantity

**5 committed members who:**
- Complete all missions
- Give honest feedback
- Feel genuinely helped

**Is infinitely more valuable than:**

**20 members who:**
- Lurk silently
- Drop off by Week 2
- Give "nice job!" feedback with no substance

**You're looking for signal, not scale.**

## Final Reminders

1. **Pick people you'd genuinely enjoy spending 4 weeks learning alongside.**
   - If the thought of checking Discord every day to see their messages feels fun ‚Üí good pick.
   - If it feels like an obligation ‚Üí probably not the right person for Season 01.

2. **It's okay if your list isn't "impressive."**
   - You don't need a chess GM, a YC founder, or a Twitter influencer.
   - You need honest humans who match your target user profile.

3. **You can always add more people mid-season.**
   - Week 2-3 you might think "oh, [person] would be perfect for this."
   - Just tell them: "Join mid-season, catch up at your own pace, Season runs until Feb 2."

4. **Trust your gut.**
   - If someone feels like the "right" person but doesn't fit a clean archetype ‚Üí invite them.
   - If someone looks perfect on paper but something feels off ‚Üí skip them.
